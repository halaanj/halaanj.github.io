Napoleon Crossing the Alps (also known as Napoleon at the Saint-Bernard Pass or Bonaparte Crossing the Alps; listed as Le Premier Consul franchissant les Alpes au col du Grand Saint-Bernard) is a collection of five oil on canvas equestrian portraits of Napoleon Bonaparte painted between 1801 and 1805 by the French artist Jacques Louis David. The composition, which was commissioned by the King of Spain, depicts a highly idealized view of Napoleon and his army's actual crossing of the Alps through the Great St Bernard Passin May 1800. It has become one of Napoleon's most widely reproduced images.Bonaparte appears mounted in a general-in-uniform, chief's wearing a gold-trimmed bicorne and wielding a Mamluk-style sabre. He is encircled by the folds of a large cloak that billows in the breeze. His head is turned to the viewer, and he motions with his right hand toward the mountain's summit. His left hand is gripping the reins of his horse. The horse stands on its hind legs, its mane and tail whipped against its body by the same wind that inflates Napoleon's cloak. A line of soldiers, interspersed with artillery, makes its way up the mountain in the background. Dark clouds hang over the image, and mountains rise sharply in front of Bonaparte.
